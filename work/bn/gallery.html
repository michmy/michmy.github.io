Life Inc.: How the World Became a Corporation and How to Take it Back

Douglas Rushkoff's new book takes on the increasingly large role of the corporation in human life, tracing its growth from the formation of the first chartered company in the Middle Ages to its dominance in today's brand-saturated world. In a culture suffused with the idea of the individual as consumer, how do we find an identity that isn't shaped by marketing?
